# Potential Challenges

This document outlines the potential challenges that author has not yet resolved and it might be a problem that raises in the future.

1. **Multi-human Multi-Agent Collaboration**: The current framework is designed for a single human and multiple AI agents. However, in a real-world scenario, there might be multiple humans collaborating with multiple AI agents. This could lead to challenges in communication, coordination, and task allocation among the humans and AI agents.

2. **Ego as a human and software engineer**: If you find yourself in a situation that "Virtual Engineers" are here to challenges your ego as a human and software engineer, it might be a challenge to accept the fact that AI agents can perform certain tasks better than humans. This could lead to resistance in adopting the framework and utilizing the virtual engineers effectively.

3. **Cost Management**: Running multiple instances of virtual engineers can be expensive, especially if the project requires a large number of AI agents. It is important to carefully consider the cost implications and optimize the usage of virtual engineers based on the project's needs and budget.

4. **Context and Information Access**: Ensuring that the virtual engineers have access to the necessary context and information to perform their tasks effectively can be a challenge. This can be addressed by implementing a robust knowledge management system that allows the virtual engineers to access relevant documentation, codebase, and project information easily. Related to Point 2, if the virtual engineers are not provided with the necessary context and information, they may produce suboptimal results or make incorrect assumptions, leading to potential issues in the project.

5. **Legacy Codebase and Inconsistent Codebase**: If the project involves working with a legacy codebase or an inconsistent codebase, it can pose challenges for the virtual engineers. They may struggle to understand the existing code structure, dependencies, and coding conventions, which can lead to difficulties in maintaining and extending the codebase. It is important to provide clear documentation and guidelines to help the virtual engineers navigate and work with the legacy or inconsistent codebase effectively.
